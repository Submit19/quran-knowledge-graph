## TL;DR — the discovery session's conclusion was wrong

The discovery session concluded "no text transcripts; Whisper-STT is the path forward." **That is incorrect.** Human-made text transcripts of Khalifa's sermons **do exist online**, just not as standalone `.txt`/HTML pages — they are **manually-uploaded English subtitle tracks** embedded in the YouTube videos on the **`@asubmitter2god`** channel.

- **58 of 90 videos** on that channel carry a **manual (human-authored) English subtitle track** — not machine ASR.
- That's **~64.6 hours** of transcript-bearing audio, an estimated **~470,000 English words** of already-transcribed Khalifa material.
- The transcripts are **high quality**: Arabic phrases transliterated *and* translated, speaker labels (`Rashad:`, `Ahmad's wife:`), proper punctuation. No ASR could produce this — it's human work.
- Most also carry a **Persian (`fa-IR`) manual track** (community translation; out of scope for the Khalifa-only English corpus but notable).

**The right next step is NOT Whisper-STT. It is a `yt-dlp` subtitle pull** (`--write-sub --sub-lang en`), which downloads the existing human transcripts directly. STT is only needed as a fallback for the 32 videos lacking a manual track — and even then, the existing human transcripts are better than any STT output.

## Verdict

**Text transcripts of Khalifa's sermons EXIST online — Y.** They are human-authored English subtitle tracks embedded in 58 YouTube videos on `@asubmitter2god`, retrievable with `yt-dlp`, totalling ~64.6 h / ~470K words. No paywall. They are NOT standalone text files (which is why the discovery session, looking only at canonical text sites, missed them).

This **supersedes** the discovery briefing's Q3 recommendation ("defer sermons to a Whisper-STT session"). STT is now a *fallback for the 32 uncovered videos*, not the primary path.

## ⚠️ Khalifa-only source rule — critical caveat for the next session

The hard rule binds: the corpus may contain only **Khalifa's own words**, not other Submitter teachers'.

Many of the 90 videos are **mixed-speaker "Quran Study" sessions** where a community member leads (titles say "by Linda", "by Kathryn", "by Lori", "by Edip", "by M. Sabahi") and Rashad interjects/teaches. The pure **"Friday Sermons by Dr. Rashad Khalifa"** videos are clean; the study sessions are not.

**Good news:** the transcripts carry **speaker labels** (`Rashad:`, plus named others), so speaker-segmentation to extract only Khalifa's spoken portions is feasible. The scrape session must segment by speaker and keep only Rashad's turns — a straight bulk ingest would violate the rule by pulling in other teachers' words.

Also: these are *transcribed/edited by the community*, not published by Khalifa. They are a faithful record of **his spoken words** (primary source = his speech), which fits the rule's "his transcribed sermons" clause — but the editorial layer (which Arabic to transliterate, where to break lines) is community work. Worth an explicit operator call.

## Three operator decisions implied by the verdict

**Decision 1 — Pull existing transcripts vs. Whisper-STT.**
- **(A) `yt-dlp` subtitle pull (RECOMMENDED).** Download the 58 manual English tracks directly. Fast (~minutes, not hours of GPU), higher quality than STT, includes speaker labels. ~470K words.
- (B) Whisper-STT the audio anyway. Slower (64+ h audio → many GPU-hours), lower quality on 1980s recordings, loses speaker labels. No reason to prefer this when human transcripts exist.
- (C) Hybrid: pull the 58 manual tracks now; STT the 32 uncovered videos later if the corpus needs them.

**Decision 2 — Khalifa-only segmentation policy for mixed sessions.**
- (A) Ingest **only the pure "Friday Sermons by Dr. Rashad Khalifa"** videos; skip mixed study sessions entirely. Safest, smallest, cleanest.
- (B) Ingest all 58 but **speaker-segment**, keeping only `Rashad:`-labelled turns. Larger corpus, requires a segmentation pass + spot-check that labels are reliable.
- (C) Ingest all 58 whole. **Violates the Khalifa-only rule** — do not do this.

**Decision 3 — Provenance / licensing of community-transcribed material.**
- (A) Treat as Khalifa-primary (his spoken words) with a provenance note that transcription/editing is community work; proceed under research fair-use, same posture as the masjidtucson.org book corpus.
- (B) Hold for explicit permission from the channel owner / USI before ingesting.
- (C) Use only as a retrieval-time grounding source, never surfaced verbatim, to sidestep the editorial-layer question.
